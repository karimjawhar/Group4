Chapter 1

Climate risk is distributed unevenly across the globe

The world map shows average climate risk score by country, revealing geographic patterns of vulnerability. Darker colors indicate higher climate risk based on factors including temperature change, extreme events, and environmental hazards.

Hover over countries to see detailed metrics. Click to filter other charts.

Chapter 2

Global population growth raises the baseline of exposure

The population dataset provides the long-run context for the story. As total population increases, more people can be affected even before we compare differences between individual countries.

Chapter 3

Population size and climate burden move together, but not evenly

The core relationship chart compares 2022 population with total affected population. Bubble size adds a third variable: average climate risk score.

Larger bubbles indicate higher average climate risk. Colors encode continent.

Chapter 5

Which countries show the strongest climate fingerprint?

This heatmap condenses six burden indicators into a single matrix, making it easier to compare countries across multiple dimensions at once rather than jumping between separate charts.

Chapter 6

Which countries move up the burden ranking?

This slope chart compares each country's population rank with its affected-population rank. It adds a more distinctive visual layer to the story by highlighting disproportionate burden, not just absolute size.

Positive shifts mean a country climbs higher in climate impact than its population rank would suggest.

Chapter 7

Regional filters reveal where total burden concentrates

The region chart introduces the project's higher-level structure. Clicking a region filters all country-level charts, making the story coherent across scales.

Chapter 8

So what does the story suggest?

The dynamic narrative below updates with your current selection so the page always explains the insight behind the filter instead of leaving interpretation entirely to the reader.

Final takeaway

Climate burden is not explained by population size alone. Comparing totals, event counts, region filters, and rank shifts helps reveal where vulnerability is concentrated and where a country climbs the burden ranking unexpectedly.

  • Use the slope chart to find disproportionate burden.
  • Use the region filter to isolate where that burden clusters.
  • Use the scatter plot and rankings together to compare scale against intensity.
